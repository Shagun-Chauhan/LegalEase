📌 Explanation
Your landlord is supposed to return your security deposit after you move out, but they haven't done it yet. This might be causing you financial stress, and you want to get your deposit back. The most important concern is getting your deposit returned in a timely manner.

📝 Steps You Can Take
Step 1: Review your lease agreement to see what it says about security deposits and return procedures.
Step 2: Send a polite and formal email or letter to your landlord, asking for the return of your deposit and providing your address and any other necessary information.
Step 3: Include a copy of the lease agreement and any other relevant documents, such as a receipt for the deposit or a letter stating the amount of the deposit.
Step 4: If you don't hear back from your landlord within a week, consider sending a follow-up email or letter to remind them of their obligation to return your deposit.
Step 5: If your landlord still refuses to return your deposit, you may need to consult with a lawyer or file a complaint with your local consumer protection agency.

❓ Follow-Up
Have you kept any records or documentation related to the security deposit, such as receipts or bank statements, that you can provide to your landlord?
